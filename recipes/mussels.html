Mussels

Description

From Melissa Clark's cookbook Dinner. Serve with oven fries and a baguette for a quick and delicious meal.

Mussels are cooked in a delicious broth of coconut milk and thai spices. The combination of flavours creates a slightly spicy, sweet, and briny broth perfect for dipping fries or bread in.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds fresh mussels
  • 2 tablespoons coconut or safflower oil
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, trimmed and finely chopped
  • ½ to 1 small hot chile (like Thai bird, Serrano, Scotch bonnet or jalapeño), seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 cup unsweetened coconut milk
  • 2 pounds fresh mussels, rinsed well
  • Zest of ½ lemon
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice, or to taste
  • ½ teaspoon Asian fish sauce, or to taste
  • ½ cup whole cilantro leaves

Steps

  1. Heat a large pot with a tight-fitting lid over medium heat. Add the oil, and when it’s hot, add the shallot, garlic, lemongrass, and chili.
  2. Cook over medium heat until soft, about 3 minutes.
  3. Add the coconut milk and mussels. Cover the pot and steam, stirring once or twice, until the mussels have opened, 5 to 7 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat, and use a slotted spoon to transfer the mussels to a large bowl, leaving the liquid in the pot. Discard any mussels that have not opened.
  5. Stir the lemon zest and juice, fish sauce and cilantro into the pot. Taste and add more fish sauce and/or lemon juice if needed (fish sauce provides the salt).
  6. Ladle the pan sauce over the mussles, and serve
